> *Every other freedom rests on a medium of exchange that can't be quietly debased. If you don't understand money, you are forced to play a game whose rules others write — and rewrite — in their favor.*

This wing takes you from "what even is money?" to understanding the monetary system well enough to make your own decisions inside it. Climb the ladder in order, or jump to whatever you need.

## The ladder

1. **ELI5** — *What money really is*, in one analogy.
2. **Basic Intro (1–3 hr)** — a guided starting point: one great video, two short primary documents, and a checklist of what you should walk away understanding.
3. **Deep Dive** — six cited entries, one per core idea:
   - Why money exists: origins & the properties of good money
   - From gold to fiat: the 1971 break
   - Banking & how money is actually created
   - Inflation & the Cantillon effect — who wins when money is printed
   - Interest & time preference
   - Hard money & Bitcoin
4. **Mastery** — projects that turn understanding into instinct: track your own purchasing power, hold value yourself, and read the primary texts.
5. **Resources** — the curated, archived shelf of books, data, and explainers.

## How to read this wing

- Entries marked **canonical** carry at least one cited source — check them; don't take my word.
- Economics has rival schools. This wing keeps the **mechanics** (how banking, inflation, and money creation actually work) separate from **interpretation** (what *should* be done about it), and flags where it leans on a particular tradition. Where reasonable people disagree, it says so.
- The goal isn't to make you an economist. It's to make you hard to fool.
