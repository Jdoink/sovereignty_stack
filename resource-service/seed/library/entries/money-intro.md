If you have one evening, spend it like this. Three sittings, roughly three hours, and you'll have a real working model of money — not vibes.

## 1. Watch (30 min) — the economy as a machine

**Ray Dalio, *How the Economic Machine Works*** ([video](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PHe0bXAIuk0)).

A clear, animated walk through credit, spending, debt cycles, and why "one person's spending is another's income." Don't worry about memorizing — just absorb the shape of the machine: transactions, credit, and the long/short debt cycles.

## 2. Read (45 min) — where money actually comes from

**Bank of England, *Money creation in the modern economy*** ([article](https://www.bankofengland.co.uk/quarterly-bulletin/2014/q1/money-creation-in-the-modern-economy)).

This is a central bank stating plainly that **most money is created by commercial banks when they make loans** — *not* by lending out a pile of existing savings, and *not* by "multiplying up" the central bank's money. It quietly demolishes the textbook story most people carry around. Read the summary and the first few pages.

## 3. Read (30 min) — the day the rules changed

**Federal Reserve History, *Nixon Ends Convertibility*** ([essay](https://www.federalreservehistory.org/essays/gold-convertibility-ends)).

On **August 15, 1971**, the U.S. stopped letting foreign governments redeem dollars for gold. From that day the dollar — and soon every major currency — became *fiat*: money backed by government decree and trust, not by a fixed amount of metal. Almost everything strange about modern money traces back to this hinge.

## You should now be able to explain…

- The three jobs money does: medium of exchange, store of value, unit of account.
- Why "banks lend out deposits" is the *wrong* mental model — and what really happens.
- What "fiat" means and what changed in 1971.
- Why printing more money doesn't make a society richer — it redistributes.
- Why one person's spending is another's income (and why credit makes the cycle).

If those click, you're ready for the **Deep Dive** tier — six entries that take each idea down to first principles.
